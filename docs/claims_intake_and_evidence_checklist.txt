A. Minimum Intake Questions (must capture)
1) Injuries: Any injuries? If yes, advise emergency services.
2) When: Date and approximate time of incident.
3) Where: City, street/area; confirm country.
4) What happened: Short description, direction of travel, point of impact.
5) Vehicles involved: number of vehicles; third-party details if available.
6) Police: Was police notified? Report/reference number (if available).
7) Drivability: Is the insured vehicle drivable? If not, towing needed.
8) Evidence: Photos/video available? Dashcam? Witnesses?

B. Recommended Evidence
- Photos: scene, vehicle damage, license plates, road signs, skid marks.
- Driver & vehicle details: names, contacts, license numbers, registration.
- Police report or reference number (if applicable).
- Repair estimate or invoice.
- Medical documentation for injury claims.

C. Safety Guidance
- If anyone is injured: prioritize medical care and emergency services.
- If vehicles are in danger: move to safe location if possible and permitted by law.
- Do not admit fault; exchange facts.

D. Next Steps for Customer
- Provide a claim number.
- Explain assessment timeline and repair options.
- Advise customer to keep receipts (towing, rental, emergency costs).
